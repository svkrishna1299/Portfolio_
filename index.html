About

I'm a Mechanical Engineer finishing my MSc at Chalmers University of Technology, where I specialise in computational fluid dynamics, turbulence modelling, and the intersection of high-fidelity simulation with machine learning.

My work spans from RANS/DES multiphase simulations of tire-water spray in heavy truck wakes, to building Fourier Neural Operator surrogates and Soft Actor–Critic reinforcement learning controllers for real-time HVAC optimisation. I'm driven by the challenge of making expensive simulations cheaper — and cheaper predictions smarter.

Before Chalmers, I spent two years as Lead Product Engineer at Turbo Energy, developing actuators and e-recirculation valves through the full product lifecycle — hands-on product development experience that shapes how I think about engineering problems.
